Tutorial: Variables

Good morning... er, what's your name again?
var name = "";




Right! Good morning,
! What you just did was declaring and assigning a variable. A variable is a container for some value that we can use whenever we want. Variables are declared with the keyword
var
. You can name them whatever you want. Variables are assigned with the equals sign (
=
). Also notice the words and text box that are colored differently. Blue text represents code, and the gray boxes with blue text are areas where you can write code.
Now that you've learned how to declare and assign variables, it's time to decide what to wear. Declare a variable,
shirtColor
, and assign it to
"red"
,
"green"
,
"blue"
, or
"yellow"
. As a refresher, this is how to declare and assign a variable:

var variableName = value;


Remember to end your line with a semicolon (
;
) Try assigning
shirtColor
to different values on different lines to see what happens.

Breakfast time! Declare variables representing how much you eat for breakfast below. You can choose from
toast
,
eggs
,
bacon
, and
waffles
. For example,

var toast = 2;


means you want to eat 2 pieces of toast. Keep in mind you can only stomach up to 5 pieces of each food, and you can't eat negative pieces. Declare and assign each variable on a separate line.


How much did you eat for breakfast? Let's create a new variable,
total
, to represent how much you ate. Make sure you add up all your breakfast variables by finishing the line below.

Wash down your breakfast with some orange juice. Start with drinking one glass.
var orangeJuice = 1;

To drink more, reassign
orangeJuice
by adding to it. For example,
orangeJuice = orangeJuice + 1;
means you drink one more glass. You only have enough orange juice left in the fridge to drink 5 total glasses.

Awesome, now you're all set to go to the amusement park!
