Objects: Fixing A Roller Coaster

The park's roller coaster, the Big Dipper, hasn't been attracting many riders. You decide to tackle the task of making the ride more popular.

You first decide to add more information to the sign advertising the Big Dipper to attract visitors. There is a variable called bigDipper, and you must include at least 3 pieces of information from that object in the sign. Click the "Need a hint?" button if you get stuck!

Hint

Take a look at this line of code:
    addLineToSign("Fun " + bigDipper.rideType);
If this is how you add a line to the sign with the rideType property of bigDipper, how could you access something else, like numLoops or speedInMPH?

Success!

Great job fixing the sign. The variable bigDipper is an object in Javascript, and rideType, name, and minRiderHeight are examples of properties of the object. Like you did to write the sign, properties of objects can be accessed like this:
    object[property]
